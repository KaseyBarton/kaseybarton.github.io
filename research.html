Research overview

How plants persist on islands

Kasey’s research integrates functional traits, experiments, field surveys, and open data to understand how plants respond to herbivores, drought, competition, and the distinctive evolutionary setting of islands.

These four projects move from individual defense traits to population dynamics and whole-forest patterns, with Hawaiʻi serving as both a remarkable natural laboratory and a place where ecological insight matters urgently.
