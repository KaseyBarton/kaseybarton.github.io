Pua kala · Argemone glauca

Project summary

Defense, built in layers

Pua kala (Argemone glauca) is Hawaiʻi’s endemic prickly poppy. Its conspicuous defenses make it a powerful system for testing a persistent idea in island biology: that island plants evolve weak defenses because they experience less pressure from herbivores.

Since 2010, the Barton Lab has examined how prickles, latex, epicuticular waxes, and tolerance vary among populations, across plant development, after herbivory, and in response to light and water availability. Together, these traits reveal a defense strategy that is dynamic rather than fixed.
