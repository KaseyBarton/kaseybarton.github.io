A genus built to defend itself

Project summary

Defense across a remarkable genus

This project examines defense in an apparently well-defended—but understudied—group of plants: the prickly poppies of the genus Argemone.

Approximately 32 Argemone species are native to the Americas. Their center of diversity lies in Mexico, while Hawaiʻi is home to a single endemic species. This unusual distribution creates a powerful natural comparison for investigating how plant defenses evolve across continental and island environments.
